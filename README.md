# Netflix-Analysis-Using-Power-BI
Netflix Power BI Dashboard – Project Explanation
1️⃣ Project Introduction

“In this project, I built an interactive Netflix data analysis dashboard using Power BI. The purpose of this project was to analyze Netflix content distribution, including movies and TV shows, across different years, ratings, and countries.”

“The dashboard helps visualize key insights such as the total number of shows, content growth over time, rating distribution, and global content availability.”

2️⃣ Key KPIs in the Dashboard
1️⃣ Total Content

KPI: 9K

Explanation:

“The first KPI shows the total number of titles available on Netflix, which is approximately 9,000 shows and movies in the dataset.”

This helps understand the overall size of Netflix’s content library.

2️⃣ Total Movies

KPI: 6K

Explanation:

“This KPI shows the total number of movies on Netflix, which is around 6,000 titles. Movies represent the majority of the Netflix content catalog.”

3️⃣ Total TV Shows

KPI: 3K

Explanation:

“This KPI represents the total number of TV shows available on Netflix, which is around 3,000 titles.”

This helps compare content distribution between movies and TV series.

3️⃣ Dashboard Visual Insights
Content Type Distribution

(Left donut chart)

“This visualization shows the percentage distribution of movies and TV shows on Netflix.”

Insights:

Movies ≈ 69%

TV Shows ≈ 30%

Conclusion:

“Movies dominate the Netflix platform compared to TV shows.”

Content Growth by Year

(Line chart)

“This chart shows how the number of movies and TV shows added to Netflix has grown over time.”

Key insight:

“We can see a significant increase in content after 2015, which reflects Netflix’s expansion and increased investment in content production.”

Total Shows by Rating

(Bar chart)

“This visualization shows the distribution of content by rating categories such as TV-MA, TV-14, PG, and others.”

Key insight:

“Most Netflix content falls under TV-MA and TV-14 ratings, which indicates that Netflix mainly targets a mature or teenage audience.”

Global Content Distribution

(Map)

“The map visual shows which countries contribute the most content to Netflix.”

Key insight:

“Countries like the United States, India, the United Kingdom, and Canada contribute a large portion of Netflix titles.”
